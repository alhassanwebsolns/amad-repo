Dubai General Knowledge

What is Dubai?

Dubai is a city located in the United Arab Emirates (UAE), which is a country in the Middle East. It is known for its modern architecture, luxury shopping, vibrant nightlife, and tourist attractions such as the Burj Khalifa, the Dubai Mall, and the Palm Jumeirah. Dubai is also a hub for business and commerce in the region.In recent years, Dubai has become a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year. The city has a diverse population, with a mix of cultures from around the world. Dubai is also known for its record-breaking attractions, such as the tallest building in the world, the Burj Khalifa, and the largest shopping mall in the world, the Dubai Mall. Additionally, the city is home to many world-class restaurants, hotels, and entertainment venues, making it a popular destination for luxury travel.

What is the Dubai national language?

The official language of Dubai and the wider United Arab Emirates is Arabic. However, English is widely spoken and understood, particularly in areas frequented by tourists and in business contexts. Hindi, Urdu, and Malayalam are also commonly spoken by expatriate communities in Dubai.

What is the historical background of Dubai?

Dubai has a rich history dating back thousands of years. The city's origins can be traced to the early 18th century when it was a small fishing and trading village. In the late 19th century, Dubai became an important trading port, with merchants from around the Arabian Gulf and as far away as India and East Africa conducting business there. In 1971, Dubai joined with six other emirates to form the United Arab Emirates (UAE), a federal state that remains in place today. Since then, Dubai has undergone significant economic and cultural development, becoming one of the fastest-growing cities in the world. Despite its modernization, Dubai has worked hard to preserve its cultural heritage and traditions, with landmarks such as the Dubai Museum and the Jumeirah Mosque showcasing the city's history and culture.

Lifestyle in Dubai

What is the currency used in Dubai?

The official currency used in Dubai is the United Arab Emirates Dirham, which is abbreviated as AED or simply Dhs. The Dirham is further divided into 100 smaller units called fils. Coins come in denominations of 1, 5, 10, 25, and 50 fils, as well as 1 Dirham. Banknotes come in denominations of 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, and 1,000 Dirhams. The Dirham has been pegged to the US dollar since 1978, with an exchange rate of 1 USD to 3.67 AED. This means that the value of the Dirham remains constant in relation to the US dollar, making it a stable currency for both locals and foreigners alike. When traveling to Dubai, it is recommended to exchange some of your home currency for Dirhams at a local exchange office or bank. Most hotels and tourist destinations also accept major credit cards such as Visa, Mastercard, and American Express. It is also worth noting that many establishments in Dubai, particularly in the more touristy areas, accept other major currencies such as the Euro and British Pound. However, it is always best to have some Dirhams on hand for smaller transactions or purchases at local markets. Overall, the Dirham is a stable and widely accepted currency in Dubai, and visitors should have no problem exchanging their home currency or using their credit cards while in the city.

What is the cost of living in Dubai?

Dubai is known to be one of the most expensive cities in the world, with a relatively high cost of living. The cost of living in Dubai can vary greatly depending on a number of factors such as accommodation, transportation, food, and entertainment. Accommodation is often the biggest expense for those living in Dubai. The cost of renting an apartment or house can vary widely depending on the location, size, and amenities of the property. It is common for expats to live in gated communities or apartment complexes that offer a range of amenities such as swimming pools, gyms, and security. The cost of rent for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center can range from 4,000 AED to 8,000 AED per month, while a three-bedroom apartment can cost between 10,000 AED to 18,000 AED per month. Transportation costs in Dubai can also be significant, particularly if you own a car. While petrol is relatively inexpensive in Dubai, the cost of purchasing and maintaining a car can add up quickly. Public transportation options such as the Dubai Metro, buses, and taxis are widely available and can be a more affordable option for those on a budget. Food and dining expenses in Dubai can vary depending on your preferences and dining choices. Dubai is known for its high-end dining options and luxury restaurants, which can be quite expensive. However, there are also plenty of affordable dining options such as local cafes and street food vendors. Grocery costs can also vary depending on the type of food you purchase, with imported goods often being more expensive than locally produced items. Entertainment expenses in Dubai can also add up quickly. While there are plenty of free or low-cost activities to enjoy, such as visiting the beach or exploring local markets, other activities such as theme parks and water parks can be quite expensive. Overall, the cost of living in Dubai can be high, but there are ways to live within your means and budget. It is important to carefully consider your expenses and prioritize your spending in order to make the most of your time in the city.

How much does a taxi cost in Dubai?

The cost of a taxi ride in Dubai varies depending on the distance and time of day. As of May 2023, the starting fare for a regular taxi is 10 AED (approximately 2.7 USD), with an additional charge of 2 AED (approximately 0.54 USD) per kilometer. There is also a surcharge of 4 AED (approximately 1.1 USD) for booking a taxi through the official Dubai Taxi app. Taxis in Dubai are generally considered safe, reliable, and readily available. However, it is important to note that during peak hours or special events, there may be increased demand and longer wait times.
