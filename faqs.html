Dubai General Knowledge

What is Dubai?

Dubai is a city located in the United Arab Emirates (UAE), which is a country in the Middle East. It is known for its modern architecture, luxury shopping, vibrant nightlife, and tourist attractions such as the Burj Khalifa, the Dubai Mall, and the Palm Jumeirah. Dubai is also a hub for business and commerce in the region. In recent years, Dubai has become a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year.

The city has a diverse population, with a mix of cultures from around the world. Dubai is also known for its record-breaking attractions, such as the tallest building in the world, the Burj Khalifa, and the largest shopping mall in the world, the Dubai Mall. Additionally, the city is home to many world-class restaurants, hotels, and entertainment venues, making it a popular destination for luxury travel.

What is the Dubai national language?

The official language of Dubai and the wider United Arab Emirates is Arabic. However, English is widely spoken and understood, particularly in areas frequented by tourists and in business contexts. Hindi, Urdu, and Malayalam are also commonly spoken by expatriate communities in Dubai.

What is the historical background of Dubai?

Dubai has a rich history dating back thousands of years. The city's origins can be traced to the early 18th century when it was a small fishing and trading village. In the late 19th century, Dubai became an important trading port, with merchants from around the Arabian Gulf and as far away as India and East Africa conducting business there.

In 1971, Dubai joined with six other emirates to form the United Arab Emirates (UAE), a federal state that remains in place today. Since then, Dubai has undergone significant economic and cultural development, becoming one of the fastest-growing cities in the world. Despite its modernization, Dubai has worked hard to preserve its cultural heritage and traditions, with landmarks such as the Dubai Museum and the Jumeirah Mosque showcasing the city's history and culture.

How is Dubai different from United Arab Emirates?

Dubai is one of the seven emirates that make up the United Arab Emirates (UAE). While Dubai is a city, the UAE is a country located in the Middle East that is made up of seven emirates, each with its own ruler and government.

Dubai is known for its modern architecture, luxury shopping, vibrant nightlife, and tourist attractions such as the Burj Khalifa, the Dubai Mall, and the Palm Jumeirah. It is a major hub for business and commerce in the region, with a diverse economy that includes industries such as tourism, finance, and logistics.

The UAE, on the other hand, is known for its oil and gas reserves, which have played a significant role in the country's economic development over the years. While the country has diversified its economy in recent years, with a focus on sectors such as tourism and renewable energy, oil and gas remain major contributors to the UAE's GDP.

In terms of government and politics, Dubai has its own government, which is responsible for managing local affairs such as transportation, infrastructure, and tourism. The UAE as a whole is governed by a federal system, with certain responsibilities such as foreign policy, defense, and finance being handled by the federal government. Each emirate has its own ruler, who is also a member of the Federal Supreme Council, which is the highest legislative and executive body in the country.

Dubai Cost of living

What is the currency used in Dubai?

The official currency used in Dubai is the United Arab Emirates Dirham, which is abbreviated as AED or simply Dhs. The Dirham is further divided into 100 smaller units called fils. Coins come in denominations of 1, 5, 10, 25, and 50 fils, as well as 1 Dirham. Banknotes come in denominations of 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, and 1,000 Dirhams.

The Dirham has been pegged to the US dollar since 1978, with an exchange rate of 1 USD to 3.67 AED. This means that the value of the Dirham remains constant in relation to the US dollar, making it a stable currency for both locals and foreigners alike.

When traveling to Dubai, it is recommended to exchange some of your home currency for Dirhams at a local exchange office or bank. Most hotels and tourist destinations also accept major credit cards such as Visa, Mastercard, and American Express. It is also worth noting that many establishments in Dubai, particularly in the more touristy areas, accept other major currencies such as the Euro and British Pound. However, it is always best to have some Dirhams on hand for smaller transactions or purchases at local markets.

Overall, the Dirham is a stable and widely accepted currency in Dubai, and visitors should have no problem exchanging their home currency or using their credit cards while in the city.

What is the cost of living in Dubai?

Dubai is known to be one of the most expensive cities in the world, with a relatively high cost of living. The cost of living in Dubai can vary greatly depending on a number of factors such as accommodation, transportation, food, and entertainment.

Accommodation is often the biggest expense for those living in Dubai. The cost of renting an apartment or house can vary widely depending on the location, size, and amenities of the property. It is common for expats to live in gated communities or apartment complexes that offer a range of amenities such as swimming pools, gyms, and security. The cost of rent for a one-bedroom apartment in the city center can range from 4,000 AED to 8,000 AED per month, while a three-bedroom apartment can cost between 10,000 AED to 18,000 AED per month.

Transportation costs in Dubai can also be significant, particularly if you own a car. While petrol is relatively inexpensive in Dubai, the cost of purchasing and maintaining a car can add up quickly. Public transportation options such as the Dubai Metro, buses, and taxis are widely available and can be a more affordable option for those on a budget.

Food and dining expenses in Dubai can vary depending on your preferences and dining choices. Dubai is known for its high-end dining options and luxury restaurants, which can be quite expensive. However, there are also plenty of affordable dining options such as local cafes and street food vendors. Grocery costs can also vary depending on the type of food you purchase, with imported goods often being more expensive than locally produced items.

Entertainment expenses in Dubai can also add up quickly. While there are plenty of free or low-cost activities to enjoy, such as visiting the beach or exploring local markets, other activities such as theme parks and water parks can be quite expensive.

Overall, the cost of living in Dubai can be high, but there are ways to live within your means and budget. It is important to carefully consider your expenses and prioritize your spending in order to make the most of your time in the city.

What is the cheapest month to visit Dubai?

Dubai is a popular tourist destination throughout the year, and the cost of travel can vary depending on various factors such as the season, festivals, and events. However, in general, the cheapest months to visit Dubai are May, June, and September.

During these months, the temperatures can be quite hot, with average highs ranging from 36°C to 41°C (96°F to 106°F), which may not be ideal for everyone. However, if you can handle the heat and humidity, you can take advantage of lower hotel rates and flight fares during these months.

It's worth noting that the holy month of Ramadan can affect the availability of certain attractions and services, as well as the operating hours of some businesses. Therefore, if you plan to visit Dubai during Ramadan (which falls on different dates each year based on the lunar calendar), it's best to check in advance and plan accordingly.

Lifestyle in Dubai

What is the dress code in Dubai?

Dubai is a city with a diverse population and culture, and while it's generally tolerant of different dress styles, it's important to be aware of the local customs and dress codes to avoid any unintentional offense.

Dress Code in Public Places

In public places, such as malls, restaurants, and tourist attractions, it's recommended to dress modestly. For men, this means wearing t-shirts, shorts, or trousers, while for women, it's recommended to dress conservatively, with clothing that covers the shoulders, upper arms, and legs at least to the knee. Bikinis and swimwear are acceptable on the beach or at swimming pools but should not be worn elsewhere.

Dress Code in Mosques

In mosques, both men and women are required to dress conservatively and cover their arms and legs. Women may also need to cover their hair with a scarf or shawl.

Dress Code During Ramadan

It's also worth noting that during the holy month of Ramadan, it's recommended to dress even more conservatively out of respect for the local customs and traditions.

Respect the Local Culture

In general, it's always a good idea to be respectful of the local culture and dress appropriately for the occasion.

What are some dos and don’ts for Dubai?

Dubai is a cosmopolitan city with a mix of cultures and traditions. Here are some general dos and don'ts to keep in mind when visiting Dubai:

Dos:

  • Dress modestly in public places and when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as avoiding public displays of affection and refraining from drinking alcohol in public.
  • Greet people with "As-salamu alaykum" (peace be upon you) and use common courtesy when interacting with locals.
  • Carry a copy of your passport and visa with you at all times as it may be required for identification purposes.
  • Bargain politely when shopping at markets or souks.

Don'ts:

  • Don't drink and drive or use drugs as they are strictly prohibited in Dubai.
  • Don't use offensive language or make insulting gestures as it may be considered a crime.
  • Don't engage in public displays of affection as it is not allowed in public places.
  • Don't photograph people without their consent, especially women.
  • Don't eat or drink in public during Ramadan, as it is a month of fasting for Muslims.

Overall, by respecting local customs, following the law, and being courteous to others, visitors can have an enjoyable and safe experience in Dubai.

What type of food do I get in Dubai? Will I get vegetarian food in Dubai?

Dubai offers a wide variety of food options to cater to the diverse population and tourist population. You can find cuisines from all over the world, including Arabic, Indian, Pakistani, Iranian, Chinese, Japanese, Italian, and American, among others.

If you are vegetarian, you can also find a good selection of vegetarian and vegan food options in Dubai. Many restaurants offer vegetarian dishes, and some even specialize in vegetarian and vegan cuisine. Some popular vegetarian dishes in Dubai include hummus, falafel, fattoush salad, tabbouleh, and lentil soup, among others. You can also find vegetarian options in international cuisines such as Italian and Indian.

It's worth noting that some traditional Arabic dishes may contain meat or meat broth, so it's important to check with the restaurant staff before ordering. Additionally, some restaurants may not have a separate kitchen or cooking area for vegetarian dishes, so there may be a risk of cross-contamination. However, many restaurants in Dubai are sensitive to dietary restrictions and are happy to accommodate special requests.

What are the tipping rules in Dubai?

Tipping is not mandatory in Dubai, but it is generally expected in most service industries. Here are some general guidelines for tipping in Dubai:

Restaurants: Many restaurants in Dubai include a service charge on the bill, which is typically 10%. If a service charge is not included, it is customary to leave a tip of 10-15% of the total bill. Some high-end restaurants may add a service charge of 15-20%. It's always a good idea to check the bill to see if a service charge has been included.

Hotels: It is common to tip hotel staff in Dubai, including housekeeping, bellhops, and concierge. A tip of 10-20 dirhams per day is customary for housekeeping, and a tip of 20-50 dirhams is typical for bellhops who assist with luggage. If a concierge goes out of their way to assist with something, a tip of 50-100 dirhams is appropriate.

Taxis: Tipping taxi drivers in Dubai is not expected, but rounding up the fare to the nearest dirham is common practice.

Other services: Tipping for other services, such as hairdressers, spa treatments, and tour guides, is generally at your discretion. A tip of 10-15% of the total cost is common.

It's worth noting that some restaurants and hotels may add a "discretionary" service charge, which is essentially a tip. If you feel that the service was not up to par, you can ask to have the service charge removed from your bill and leave a smaller tip or no tip at all.

Can you hold hands in public in Dubai?

Public displays of affection, including holding hands, are generally not allowed in Dubai. The UAE follows Islamic laws and customs, and such behavior is considered inappropriate in public. However, it is not illegal, and some couples do hold hands in public. It's always a good idea to be mindful of local customs and practices when traveling to a new place.

Can you take alcohol into Dubai?

It is illegal to bring alcohol into Dubai or the UAE unless you have a permit. The UAE has strict laws regarding alcohol consumption and possession, and individuals caught violating these laws can face severe penalties, including fines, imprisonment, and deportation. However, alcohol is widely available in Dubai in licensed hotels, restaurants, and bars.

How do I buy a local Sim Card? Will I be able to get Wi-Fi connectivity?

You can buy a local SIM card at the airport or at any of the major malls or shopping centers in Dubai. The two main telecommunications providers in Dubai are Etisalat and du. You will need to bring your passport with you to purchase a SIM card.

Once you have a local SIM card, you will have access to mobile data and can use your phone to connect to Wi-Fi networks. Many hotels, cafes, and public areas in Dubai offer free Wi-Fi, so it is relatively easy to stay connected while you are in the city. You can also purchase a portable Wi-Fi device or a local data plan from your telecom provider if you need more reliable internet access.

What is it like during Ramadan (Islamic Sacred Month) in Dubai?

Ramadan is a significant religious event for Muslims around the world, and it is no different in Dubai. During Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn to sunset, abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs. In Dubai, non-Muslims are not required to fast, but they are expected to respect the customs and traditions of the holy month.

The atmosphere in Dubai during Ramadan is unique, and visitors may notice some changes in daily life. Many restaurants and cafes are closed during the day, but they usually stay open late into the night. Non-Muslims are not allowed to eat, drink, or smoke in public during the day, but they can do so in designated areas.

There is a festive atmosphere in Dubai during Ramadan, with many events and activities taking place throughout the month. Visitors can attend iftar (breaking of fast) gatherings and enjoy traditional Arabic cuisine. Dubai also hosts various cultural events, exhibitions, and musical performances during the month.

The city usually comes alive after sunset, and it is not unusual to see people out late into the night. Many of the malls, shops, and tourist attractions stay open late, making it an excellent time to explore the city.

Overall, visitors to Dubai during Ramadan should be respectful of the customs and traditions of the holy month, but they can still enjoy the unique atmosphere and festivities that come with it.

Are there any photography rules in Dubai?

Yes, there are certain photography rules that should be followed in Dubai. It is important to respect the local culture and traditions when taking photographs in public places. Some guidelines to follow include:

  1. Avoid taking pictures of government buildings, military installations, and airports.
  2. Do not take pictures of people without their permission, especially women and children.
  3. Avoid taking pictures of mosques during prayer times.
  4. Respect people's privacy and do not take pictures of private property without permission.
  5. Avoid taking pictures of accidents or emergencies, as this can be considered disrespectful.
  6. Do not take pictures of local women without their permission, as this can be considered disrespectful and offensive.
  7. It is also important to note that drone photography is strictly prohibited in certain areas of Dubai, so be sure to check the rules and regulations before flying a drone.

By following these guidelines, visitors can ensure that they are respectful of local customs and traditions when taking photographs in Dubai.

Safety & Emergencies

Is Dubai safe to travel to for tourists?

Yes, Dubai is generally considered a safe destination for tourists. The UAE has a low crime rate, and the government places a strong emphasis on ensuring the safety and security of visitors. However, like any major city, it's important to take certain precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas or tourist spots. Petty theft can occur, so it's advisable to keep valuables safe and secure. Additionally, it's important to respect local laws and customs, as the UAE has strict regulations on issues such as public displays of affection, alcohol consumption, and dress code. Overall, as long as visitors exercise common sense and follow the rules and regulations, Dubai is a safe and enjoyable destination for tourists.

Is Dubai Police helpful?

Yes, Dubai Police are known to be very helpful and efficient. They are well-trained and equipped to handle various types of emergencies and crimes. They also have a strong presence in public areas, which helps maintain law and order in the city. Additionally, the Dubai Police have a reputation for being polite and respectful towards residents and tourists.

What do I do in case I lost valuable item in Dubai?

If you lose a valuable item in Dubai, you should report the loss to the local police station as soon as possible. You will need to provide details about the lost item, including a description, the location where it was lost, and the time and date. It is also a good idea to provide any identifying information or serial numbers for the lost item, if applicable.

In addition to reporting the loss to the police, you can also check with any nearby lost and found offices or contact the management of the location where the item was lost to see if it has been turned in. You can also post about the lost item on social media or classified websites to increase your chances of finding it.

If you have travel insurance, you should also contact your insurance provider to report the loss and see if you are eligible for any reimbursement. It is important to keep any documentation related to the loss, such as police reports or receipts for the lost item, as this may be required by your insurance provider.

Many public buildings, transportation services, and tourist attractions have been designed or adapted to cater to the needs of people with disabilities. For instance, Dubai Municipality has developed comprehensive guidelines for designing public spaces to be more accessible for people with disabilities, including wheelchair users and people with visual and hearing impairments.

Overall, while there is still room for improvement, Dubai is working to become a more inclusive and special needs-friendly destination.

Weather

What is the best time to visit Dubai?

The best time to visit Dubai is between November and April, when the weather is milder and more pleasant. During this time, temperatures are usually in the mid-20s to low-30s Celsius (70s to 80s Fahrenheit), and there is less humidity and fewer chances of rain. This period is also considered the peak tourist season, with many festivals and events taking place, such as the Dubai Shopping Festival and the Dubai World Cup.

From May to October, Dubai experiences very hot and humid weather, with temperatures reaching up to 40°C (104°F) and higher. This period is also known as the off-season, with fewer tourists and lower prices on hotels and flights. However, outdoor activities can be challenging during this time, and it is important to stay hydrated and avoid prolonged exposure to the sun.

Overall, the best time to visit Dubai depends on your preferences and budget. If you want to enjoy milder weather and attend popular events, it is best to visit between November and April. If you prefer lower prices and don't mind the heat, you can consider visiting during the off-season from May to October.

What is the weather like in Dubai?

Dubai has a desert climate, with hot and sunny weather throughout most of the year. The hottest months are from May to September, when temperatures can reach up to 45°C (113°F) or higher during the day. Humidity is also high during these months, making the weather feel even hotter.

From November to April, the weather in Dubai is milder and more pleasant, with temperatures ranging from the mid-20s to low-30s Celsius (70s to 80s Fahrenheit). This period is also known as the peak tourist season, with many outdoor festivals and events taking place.

Overall, Dubai has a year-round sunny and dry climate, with very little rainfall. It is important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun, especially during the hotter months.

What is it like during summer in Dubai?

During summer in Dubai, the weather is extremely hot and humid, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F) and occasionally reaching up to 45°C (113°F) or higher. The summer months in Dubai are from May to September, and during this period, there is very little relief from the heat and humidity, even at night. It can be uncomfortable to be outside during the day, and it is important to stay hydrated and avoid prolonged exposure to the sun. Many indoor activities, such as shopping malls and indoor attractions, have air conditioning to help provide relief from the heat. It is also important to note that during the summer months, there is a higher risk of sandstorms and occasional thunderstorms.

What is the coldest month of Dubai?

Dubai experiences a hot desert climate, so temperatures in the city rarely drop below 20°C (68°F) even in the coldest months. However, the months of December and January are generally considered the coolest months in Dubai, with average high temperatures of around 24°C (75°F) and low temperatures of around 14°C (57°F).

Travel Basics

Do I need a visa to visit Dubai?

It depends on your nationality and the purpose and duration of your visit to Dubai.

If you are a citizen of one of the countries that are eligible for visa-on-arrival or visa-free entry, then you do not need to apply for a visa in advance. You can simply obtain a visa upon arrival at the Dubai airport.

If you are not eligible for visa-on-arrival or visa-free entry, then you will need to apply for a visa before you travel to Dubai. You can apply for a visa through the official website of the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) or through a local UAE embassy or consulate.

It's important to note that visa requirements can change frequently, so it's always a good idea to check with the relevant authorities before you travel.

What are the prohibited goods to carry in Dubai?

Dubai has strict rules and regulations regarding prohibited items that travelers are not allowed to bring into the country. Some of the prohibited goods that are not allowed to be carried in Dubai include:

  • Illegal drugs and narcotics
  • Weapons and firearms (unless you have obtained the necessary permits)
  • Explosives and fireworks
  • Pornographic material
  • Religious and politically sensitive material
  • Counterfeit goods
  • Endangered species and products made from them
  • Fruits, vegetables, and other agricultural products without the necessary permits

It's important to note that the list of prohibited goods in Dubai is not limited to the items mentioned above. The UAE government has strict regulations on what can and cannot be brought into the country, so it's always a good idea to check with the relevant authorities before you travel to Dubai.

What do I need to bring when visiting Dubai?

When visiting Dubai, there are several essential items you should bring with you, including:

  • Passport and visa (if required): Make sure to check the visa requirements for your nationality and ensure that your passport is valid for at least six months from the date of entry.
  • Travel insurance: While not mandatory, it's recommended to have travel insurance to cover any unforeseen events that may occur during your trip.
  • Currency: The official currency of Dubai is the United Arab Emirates Dirham (AED). It's recommended to carry some local currency with you for expenses such as transportation, food, and souvenirs.
  • Suitable clothing: Dubai is a Muslim country, and it's important to dress modestly. Women should avoid wearing revealing clothing, and men should avoid wearing shorts in public places.
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses: Dubai is a sunny destination, and it's essential to protect your skin and eyes from the strong sun.
  • Adapters and chargers: Dubai uses a Type G plug, so make sure to bring an adapter if your electronics have a different plug type.
  • Medications: If you're taking any medications, make sure to bring enough for your entire trip and pack them in your carry-on bag.
  • Comfortable shoes: Dubai has many attractions and activities, so bring comfortable shoes for walking and exploring.

Remember to check the weather forecast and plan accordingly to ensure you bring the appropriate clothing and gear for your trip.

Transportation

How do I get around Dubai?

To get around Dubai, you have several transportation options available:

  • Public transportation: Dubai has an extensive public transportation system, including buses, the Dubai Metro, and the Dubai Tram. The Dubai Metro is a driverless train system that covers most of the city, including the airport. The Dubai Tram connects the Dubai Marina with the Palm Jumeirah and Al Sufouh. Public transportation is an affordable and convenient way to get around Dubai.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available throughout Dubai and are relatively inexpensive compared to other major cities. All taxis are metered, and tipping is not expected but appreciated.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a popular option for those who want more flexibility and independence when exploring Dubai. Most major car rental companies have locations throughout the city, and driving is on the right side of the road.
  • Private transportation: Private transportation services such as Uber and Careem are available in Dubai and can be a convenient option for those who prefer not to use public transportation or taxis.
  • Walking and cycling: Dubai has many pedestrian-friendly areas, and walking or cycling can be a great way to explore the city, especially in areas like Dubai Marina and Jumeirah Beach.

Overall, Dubai has a reliable and efficient transportation system, and travelers have many options to choose from when getting around the city.

Where can I take a metro in Dubai?

You can take the Dubai Metro at various locations throughout the city. The Dubai Metro has two lines: the Red Line and the Green Line.

The Red Line runs from Rashidiya in the north to Jebel Ali in the south and stops at key locations such as Dubai International Airport, Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, and Dubai Marina.

The Green Line runs from Etisalat in the east to Dubai Healthcare City in the west and stops at key locations such as Dubai Creek, Dubai Museum, and Dubai Festival City.

Both lines intersect at two stations: Union and BurJuman. The Dubai Metro is a convenient and affordable way to get around the city and is a popular mode of transportation for tourists and locals alike.
